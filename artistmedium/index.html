Favorite Mediums

While I primarily work with oil paints, I also enjoy experimenting with watercolors and acrylics. Each medium offers unique challenges and opportunities for self-expression.

 

Acrylic Paint: Acrylics are versatile and fast-drying. They can be used for a wide range of techniques, from thin washes to thick impasto.

Oil paints are known for their richness and blending capabilities. They have a slower drying time, which allows for more intricate work and layering.

 

Watercolors are transparent paints that are often used for delicate and expressive works. They are known for their luminosity and are applied with water.

Gouache is similar to watercolor but more opaque. It's popular for its ability to be layered and reworked. Pastels come in various forms, including soft pastels, oil pastels, and hard pastels. They offer vibrant color and are applied directly onto the surface.

 

Ink can be used for both drawing and painting. It can create bold, flowing lines or delicate, detailed work. Artists often combine multiple mediums in a single artwork, creating unique textures and visual effects.

 

Digital Painting: With the advent of digital technology, artists can create paintings using software and graphic tablets, allowing for endless possibilities and easy editing.
